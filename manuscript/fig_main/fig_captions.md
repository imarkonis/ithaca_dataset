## Figure 1 — Conceptual methodology diagram
**Caption:** Five coherent precipitation–evaporation analysis pairs provide the sampling inputs, while separate eight-member precipitation and evaporation reference ensembles are used only for evaluation. PET products support physical-plausibility screening, and GRACE, CCI, and ROBIN enter as auxiliary validation datasets rather than as inputs to the main workflow. All products are harmonised to a common 0.25° annual grid for 1982–2021 using the `twc_complete_grid` mask. Dataset performance is evaluated from climatology, variability, trend, significance agreement, and physical consistency, and grid-cell scores are aggregated into IPCC AR6 region × biome selection probabilities. The base weighting scenario defines the principal Monte Carlo pathway, while seven alternative scenarios provide sensitivity records. For each ensemble member, one dataset is sampled within each region × biome unit. The resulting probabilistic ensemble represents plausible terrestrial water-cycle states rather than a single merged estimate and provides P, E, P−E water availability, (P+E)/2 water flux, and storyline products at grid, region × biome, regional, and global scales.
